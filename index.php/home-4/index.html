18th International Conference

Role of Higer Education in National / International Development

February 4 - 6, Coimbatore, India

The Association of Indian College Principals

The AICP has been formed in 1997 with an aim of creating new trends in the higher education system of India. It serves as a platform for the principals to express their views on the current trends of higher education. This Association promotes academic and administrative excellence in the field of higher education. The motto of this association is ‘mind without fear’. The AICP has organized seventeen national conferences for the Principals throughout the country. The AICP jointly organizes its 18th International Conference with Sri Krishna Arts and Science College from 4th-6th February of 2017.

Theme of the Conference

There is a rapidly growing demand for higher education in today’s world. It is in need of radical reforms. The conference addresses an important issue in the international educational context. The adage of this conference is ‘Courage to Lead’. Educational leadership is to challenge, interpret and develop theory with regard to higher education from different perspectives. It has to ascertain changes at all levels in Higher Education system.

Sri Krishna Arts and Science College

Sri Krishna Arts and Science College is an autonomous institution affiliated to Bharathiar University, Coimbatore founded in the year 1997. The Institution is housed in an area of 14.17 acres of land with the state of art buildings of about 34,148 Sq.mts. The Institution has established a quality management system which is certified by TUV SUD (Germany), ISO 9001:2008 standard. The College is reaccredited at “A” grade with the high score of CGPA of 3.26 by NAAC in the year 2015-2016. The Week magazine has ranked the College in the 45th position among the Commerce Colleges in India. At present the College has 23 UG programmes, 12 PG programmes, 1 Integratedprogramme and 7 Research programmes with 6,097 students as its strength.

Thrust Areas

The thrust areas for discussion are as follows

New Education Policy

Changes in Higher Education to support ‘Make in India’

Promoting entrepreneurship through colleges effectively

The role of finishing schools in Higher Education

Contribution of College Education to National Development – My Experiments

“Start Ups” through colleges

Role of colleges in Promoting Social Awareness

The conference aims to bring together principals and administrators of various higher educational institutions. It assists to exchange and share their experiences on all aspects of Higher Education. The vision of the conference is to collaborate, innovate and transform the professional learning.

Message

It is delighting to know that Association of Indian College Principals (A1CP) is organizing its 18th International Conference on "Role of Higher Education in National/International Development". Needless to say, since its inception, AICP has been tirelessly engaged in championing the cause of higher education in India by providing a common platform to the educational administrators of the country to assemble and deliberate the relevant issues and concerns affecting the scenario of higher education in India vis-à-vis International developments. The AICP deserves compliments for its commendable contribution and initiatives in the field of higher education in the country. The seventeen conferences it has organized so far provided immense opportunities for the stakeholders of higher education in India to share and consolidate their collective wisdom.

I am sure that with the constructive participation and contribution. A1CP shall emerge as a catalyst of desired change in higher education institutions across the country. The ambitious concepts like Choice -Based Credit System (CBCS), enhanced use of Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs), Global Initiative of Academic Networks ((IAN), Digital India and Skill India campaigns can be successfully implanted and practiced by developing an understanding among institutions at national level and, therefore, AICP has a greater role ahead. I feel that with its pan -Indian identity, it can develop a consensus among the diverse voices influenced by various regional, socio- cultural and linguistic contexts so that efforts of the governments and policy makers may be oriented towards the select key areas which need to be addressed on priority. lt will certainly pave the way for restoring and reviving the lost glory of higher education institutions in India. With a vast network of 799 Universities, 39071 colleges and 11923 Stand Alone Institutions in the country (Source: MHRD), the role of AICP becomes pivotal in maintaining connect among the institutions so that the objective of inclusive quality higher education may be realized. Being an effective organization, Association of Indian College Principals may expand its horizon by focusing on the relevant issues like skilling the youth to harness the demographic dividend, transforming the Indian higher education institutions as top -rankers in the global ranking, and overpowering adversity and diversity obstructing the higher education aspirants. Lastly, I wish the AICP and Sri Krishna Arts and Science College, Coimbatore the best of success for the International Conference. I believe that the Conference shall come out with the meaningful discussions on higher education in India and abroad.

Good luck!
